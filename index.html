Nervous System

Control • Communication • Direction (Brain • Spinal cord • Nerves)

Inner Diviner • Orí guidance
Nervous system overview (diagram).

Practice (3 steps)

  • SGI Pillar

    Infinite Wisdom (Tehuti) — perception, interpretation, decision

  • Khemetian Framing

    Tehuti (Divine Intelligence) • The “command center” of alignment

  • Ifá / Òrìṣà Correspondence

    Orí (higher consciousness & destiny navigation) • Crossroads of signals and choices

  • Key Teaching

    The nervous system is the body’s oracle network: signal → meaning → direction.

Reflection: When stressed, does your “inner diviner” become noisy, scattered, or clear?

2

Circulatory (Cardiovascular) System

Transport • Life-flow (Heart • Blood • Vessels)

Devotional Flow • Life distribution
Circulatory system (arteries & veins).

Practice (3 steps)

  • SGI Pillar

    Radical Devotion (Auset) — devotion as consistent life-flow

  • Khemetian Framing

    Auset (Devotional Heart) • love as sustaining circulation

  • Ifá / Òrìṣà Correspondence

    Ẹjẹ (blood as covenant of life) • movement, sweetness, and distribution

  • Key Teaching

    Whatever you “feed” emotionally circulates—devotion strengthens; bitterness thickens.

Reflection: What circulates most in your inner world—peace, worry, gratitude, anger?

3

Respiratory System

Breath • Oxygen exchange (Airways • Lungs • Diaphragm)

Breath & Vibration • Speech-as-breath
Respiratory system (airways & lungs).

Practice (3 steps)

  • SGI Pillar

    Words of Power (Sekert) — speech as structured breath

  • Khemetian Framing

    Breath principle (air, rhythm, life-force) • calm = control

  • Ifá / Òrìṣà Correspondence

    Ẹmi (breath/spirit) • vibration, prayer, incantation

  • Key Teaching

    Breath is the bridge: body ↔ mind ↔ spirit. Change the breath, change the state.

Reflection: When you’re triggered, what does your breathing do—tighten, rush, or soften?

4

Digestive System

Breakdown • Absorption • Elimination (Mouth • Stomach • Intestines • Liver)

Transformation • Matter-to-life
Digestive tract (overview).

Practice (3 steps)

  • SGI Pillar

    Radical Devotion expressed as discipline • nourishment as ritual

  • Khemetian Framing

    Earth/Matter principle — refining density into usable power

  • Ifá / Òrìṣà Correspondence

    Aṣẹ (transformative power) • what you ingest becomes what you are

  • Key Teaching

    Digestion is alchemy: input → transformation → assimilation → release.

Reflection: What “foods” (literal or emotional) do you tolerate that your system rejects?

5

Musculoskeletal System

Structure • Protection • Movement (Bones • Muscles • Joints)

Foundation • Strength • Ancestral structure
Skeletal framework (front view).
Muscular system (overview).

Practice (3 steps)

  • SGI Principle

    Standing on the shoulders of enlightened ancestors — bones as ancestral architecture

  • Khemetian Framing

    Ausar (structure, stability, enduring form) • the body’s “royal frame”

  • Ifá / Òrìṣà Correspondence

    Igbẹ́yà (foundation/strength) • discipline, readiness, protective power

  • Key Teaching

    A strong foundation makes higher work possible. Weak structure makes spiritual practice unstable.

Reflection: Where do you “collapse” in life—posture, boundaries, consistency, or courage?
